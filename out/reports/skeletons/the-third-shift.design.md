## Structure summary

New hire Wren Calloway's first "third shift" at the Meridian Annex, a
records complex that wakes only 11:00 PM to 5:44 AM. Informational sort into
Security, Archives, or Maintenance; the same one-night wrongness (the
reconciliation that has run one short since a worker, Case, never signed
out) seen through three disciplined facets.

## Review verdict (7 categories): all PASS, OVERALL APPROVE

- **Rhythm difference confirmed programmatically** (the decisive category):
  Security front-loaded (a ternary at d6, all three children re-fork at d7,
  fast-fail exits at d8, deep payoffs d19-21); Archives steady all-binary
  cadence (forks evenly d7-22); Maintenance a comb down a spine (d7/13/19/24)
  ending in a 4-way at d31. Ending-depth fingerprints are structurally
  distinct, not re-skins.
- **POV discipline clean**: Security = cameras refuse to record; Archives =
  records rewritten/pre-filled; Maintenance = systems serve something else.
  The one-night timeline (1:14 eleven-minute reset, count sweeping to
  Sub-Level 3, 3:00 elevator stop, 5:44 unlock) is consistent across all
  three tracks; the amber count is deliberately visible to each through its
  own facet (the shared wrongness, not a leak).
- **Floor (PL-20)**: shortest satisfying completion is depth 20; no
  success/completion leaf below 18.
- **Band fit**: violence mild, scariness/peril intense (at ceiling). The
  single death-kind ending is off-page release, no gore; the depth-8
  fast-fail captures read as eerie dignified conclusions, not punishment
  shrugs.

## Judgment note (recorded, no change required)

The designer's self-report claimed *every* negative ending is gated by a
telegraphed rule-break; the reviewer verified only 5 of 11 are (the
captures plus the breaker shortcut). The other 6 are intentional quiet
compliance/inaction/authored-conclusion exits (dignified melancholy), which
is good design; only the self-report's blanket phrasing overstated. No node
fix needed.
