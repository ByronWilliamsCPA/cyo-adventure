## Structure summary

Pure tree (reviewer-verified in-degree 1 everywhere). Setup spine
`n_park -> n_slip -> n_run -> n_fork` (3 setup nodes), then a pond world and a
hill world that never share props. Six endings, path lengths 11-12 nodes
(floor 7): three catches, one kite-flavored success, and two loss-reframes
(duck-parade discovery; strawberry-cone goodbye with Grandpa's hug and a
"pick a new balloon together" promise). Grandpa is present on every branch.

## Review verdict (7 categories)

1. Arc quality: PASS (loss endings land wistful-but-comforted, not sad)
2. Choice meaningfulness: PASS, with a recorded spec deviation: decisions per
   path are 2-3 (all 2-way) vs the 3-5 aim; structurally forced by the
   6-ending budget in a pure tree and judged developmentally fine for 3-5
3. Branch independence: PASS (no prop leakage, no starved branches)
4. Reconvergence readability: PASS (pure tree confirmed)
5. Band policy fit: PASS (peril rides the balloon, never the child; helper
   adults are friendly tropes with Grandpa always present)
6. Fillability: PASS (each decision's beats set up exactly its two labels)
7. Diversity: PASS (committed chase/letting-go vs teddy picnic's
   hub-gathering)

OVERALL: APPROVE, no fixes required.
