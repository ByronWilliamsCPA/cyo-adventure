## Structure summary

Setup `n_start -> n_brief`, informational 3-way sort into RADIO / MAPS /
FIELD KIT tracks that never touch again. Same storm from three angles with
shared world anchors (attic net hub, Bell Creek, Route 9, fairgrounds).
25 endings (9 success, 7 completion, 3 discovery, 6 adult-cushioned
setbacks placed only after corner-cutting choices). Floor path 13 nodes
(floor 12); ~4 decisions per path, the pure-tree equilibrium at this
ending budget.

## Review verdict (7 categories): all PASS, OVERALL APPROVE

Two non-blocking fixes applied in the polish cycle (supervisor-verified):

1. `content_flags.scariness` raised mild -> moderate for honest declaration
   (lost-hiker subplot, funnel-cloud scare, rocking van), within ceiling.
2. `m_b_wrong` reconciled to one canonical fact sheet (quarry hail stale,
   Bell Creek live, fairgrounds gauge feed lagging), removing a
   cross-branch ground-truth contradiction while preserving the ending's
   lesson (erasing uncertainty, not picking the wrong station).

## Pattern-setter rules recorded for future sorting_hat briefs

1. Shared setup teaches rules/stakes; the sort node names each track's job
   and lead character before the pick.
2. Cross-track world anchors are the payoff, governed by ONE canonical fact
   sheet per anchor (the Bell Creek flip is the failure mode).
3. Expect 4-5 decisions per path; make each count (branch-shaping,
   both-succeed presentation choices, honest-setback safety choices).
4. Negative endings only after corner-cutting choices, adult-cushioned,
   doctrine-teaching.

## Notes for the future fill stage

Fill track by track with the anchor fact sheet pinned in the preamble
(attic net, Bell Creek = hail, Route 9 = gusts, fairgrounds = festival +
lagging gauge, quarry = power flash); the anchors must sound like one storm
from three vantages.
