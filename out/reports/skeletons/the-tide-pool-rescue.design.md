## Structure summary

A forward spine of four rescue set-pieces (sea star, fish, crab,
periwinkle), each a verified loop SCC (plan -> comic flop -> retry / Gran
tip -> win) that exits only through its win node, with the winning plan
always visible on first visit. Two early-out setback endings (after rescues
1 and 3), then a tide-returns finale fanning to 9 warm endings. Shortest
satisfying completion 30 nodes (floor 9); decisions ~8 per full path,
above the 4-6 aim (recorded deviation; two are lightweight rest beats).

## Review verdict (7 categories)

1. Arc quality: PASS (weakest seam: rescues 1 and 4 mechanically similar;
   escalation flavor added in repair)
2. Choice meaningfulness: PASS (flops are fun-to-pick comedy, not traps)
3. Loop soundness: PASS (all four SCCs verified; no forced flops)
4. Reconvergence readability: PASS (consumables handled: refillable bucket,
   pocketed spoon)
5. Band policy fit: PASS after one required fix (see repairs); water safety
   and animal handling otherwise exemplary (wet hands for the sea star,
   scoop-water-with-fish, bare-hand grab fails by design)
6. Fillability: PASS
7. Diversity: PASS (chain-of-set-pieces is a genuine third 5-8 rhythm vs
   hub-gather and time_cave)

Initial overall: REVISE (two beat-level edits).

## Repair cycle 1 (fable, same designer; supervisor-verified)

1. `pw_flop_roll`: the roll-the-snail gag no longer depicts a live snail
   rolling; Winnie tucks tight and will not budge, and the retry label
   became "Smile at the stubborn shell and try again." (imitable-handling
   risk removed).
2. `e_tide_star`: self-reproach clause replaced with guilt-free warmth
   ("one rescue by Milo and three by the sea... what a fine team").
3. Optional escalation applied: `pw_win` now frames rescue 4 as the
   longest, highest, most careful carry of the day.

## Notes for the future fill stage

Plan and Gran-tip nodes carry explicit revisit-safe instructions; keep them
visit-neutral in prose. Never narrate the bucket or spoon as spent. The
tide must stay a friend (sparkle and momentum, no dread).
